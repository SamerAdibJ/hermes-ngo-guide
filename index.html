🎮 How to Play

Simple steps for each duel.

1

Pick a Challenge

One of us proposes a task. Realistic or absurd — doesn't matter. E.g. "Write a polite rejection email to a donor," "Explain blockchain to a 10-year-old," "Create a 1-week meal plan for under $50."

2

Both Craft a Prompt

Each person writes a prompt for their Hermes agent. No collaborating — pure individual creativity. Spend as much or as little time as you want.

3

Run & Capture

Each runs their prompt against their own Hermes agent. Save the output exactly as it appears — no editing, no cherry-picking.

4

Judge & Document

Decide together who wins, or call it a tie. Then add the duel to this page via GitHub.

🔄 How to update this page: Fork/edit the repo at github.com/SamerAdibJ/hermes-ngo-guide → edit index.html → add a new duel section → push. The page auto-updates on GitHub Pages. Both of you can be added as collaborators.

📜 Rules

Keeps it fun and fair.

🕐

One Prompt, One Shot

Each person writes one prompt and runs it once. No tweaking after seeing the result. The output is the output.

📸

No Editing Outputs

The raw agent response is what gets published. Typo for typo. If it hallucinated a fact, that's part of the record.

🤝

Winner by Consensus

Both agree who won, or it's a tie. No arguing. If you can't agree — call it a tie and learn from both.

🎭

Any Challenge Welcome

Serious (NGO reports) or silly (write a poem as a pirate). The variety is the fun part.

⚠️ Note on fairness: Samer runs DeepSeek (cloud, powerful). Friend runs a local model on RTX 5080. Different hardware and models means different strengths — that's the point. It's not about whose setup is better, it's about who crafts the better prompt for their specific agent.
