Getting Started with Hermes Agent

A friendly, visual guide for NGO professionals who want to work smarter — not harder — using AI agents for communication, reporting, events, and daily office workflows.

👋 What is Hermes Agent?

Think of it as a personal assistant that lives in your computer — one you can talk to, give tasks to, and who remembers how you like things done.

💬

You talk to it

Type what you need — "Draft a polite reminder email about tomorrow's meeting" — and it does the work.

🧠

It understands context

Unlike a simple chatbot, it can follow multi-step instructions, remember what you said before, and ask clarifying questions.

🛠️

It takes action

Write to files, send emails, search the web, run scripts, generate reports. It doesn't just answer — it does.

📱

Works anywhere

Use it from a desktop app, Telegram, WhatsApp, or even SMS. Same assistant, wherever you are.

✓ The big idea: Instead of switching between tabs — email, calendar, notes, ChatGPT, browser — you give one instruction and Hermes handles the chain of tasks. One prompt. Multiple actions. Less busywork.

🤔 What is an AI Agent?

A simple way to understand the difference between a chatbot and an agent.

💭

Chatbot (like ChatGPT)

You ask a question, it gives an answer. That's it. You still have to copy-paste, open other apps, and do the actual work yourself.

"Write an email for me" → it writes text, and you copy it to Gmail yourself.

🤖

AI Agent (Hermes)

You give it a goal. It figures out the steps, uses tools, and delivers the finished result. It can write files, send messages, search the web, and chain multiple actions together.

"Send a polite reminder about tomorrow's meeting to the team" → it writes the email and sends it.

💡 Analogy: A chatbot is like a recipe book that tells you how to cook. An AI agent is like a chef who cooks the meal for you — you just say what you want.
📋

Example 1 — Daily admin

Chatbot: "Write a meeting summary for me." → gives text, you paste it somewhere.
Agent: "Summarise today's meeting notes and save them to the report folder." → writes the file for you.

📧

Example 2 — Emails

Chatbot: "Draft a reply to the donor's email." → gives you text, you copy into Outlook.
Agent: "Reply to the latest email from [donor] saying we received their report and will respond by Friday." → finds the email and sends the reply.

📊

Example 3 — Reporting

Chatbot: "Give me a structure for a quarterly report." → gives you an outline.
Agent: "Create a quarterly report draft from these bullet points and save it as a .docx." → creates the actual document.

🛠️ Installation & Setup

Getting Hermes running on your computer takes about 15–20 minutes. Here's what you need and how to do it.

📦 What you need before starting:
• A computer (Windows, Mac, or Linux) — any laptop is fine
• An internet connection
• An API key from a provider like OpenAI, Anthropic, or DeepSeek (costs a few dollars per month)
• Optional: a Telegram or WhatsApp account (for mobile use)
1

Install Node.js

Go to nodejs.org and download the LTS (Long Term Support) version. Run the installer — it's like installing any other program. This gives Hermes a runtime environment to run on.

2

Install Git

Go to git-scm.com, download, and install. This is a tool for downloading code from the internet.

3

Install Hermes Agent

Open your terminal (Command Prompt on Windows, or Terminal on Mac) and type:

npx hermes-agent setup

Follow the on-screen instructions. The installer will guide you through everything.

4

Get an API Key

Hermes needs a "brain" to think — that's the AI model. Sign up at a provider like OpenAI or Anthropic, create an API key (a long secret code), and paste it into Hermes during setup.

5

Verify it works

Type your first prompt:

hello, who are you?
⚠️ Note: Some steps may vary depending on your operating system (Windows vs Mac) or the version of Hermes you're installing. If something doesn't work, always refer to the official Hermes documentation at hermes-agent.nousresearch.com/docs — it has the most up-to-date instructions.

💼 Practical Use Cases for Your Role

Real things you can do with Hermes from day one — no coding required.

📧

Draft professional emails

Ask it to write a formal email to a partner, donor, or government office. Specify the tone — diplomatic, urgent, polite.

📝

Summarise meetings

Paste your messy meeting notes and say "Turn this into a clean summary with action items."

📄

Prepare NGO reports

Give it bullet points from a field visit and ask for a structured report in professional language.

🗓️

Organise event tasks

"Create a step-by-step checklist for our workshop next Thursday with deadlines and responsible persons."

📢

Communication plans

"Draft a 1-page communication plan for our upcoming campaign targeting youth volunteers."

⏰

Reminders & follow-ups

"Remind me to check on the donor report every Monday at 9 AM until it's submitted."

🎯

Rewrite for diplomacy

Paste a strongly worded email and ask: "Rewrite this more diplomatically."

🌐

Translate & improve

"Improve this Arabic paragraph to be more professional" or "Translate this English draft to French for our partners."

📱

Social media posts

"Write 3 LinkedIn announcement posts about our recent workshop. Keep the tone inspiring but professional."

✅

Checklists & templates

"Create a workshop preparation checklist with columns: task, deadline, person responsible, status."

🔑 Key principle: If you do a repetitive task more than twice a week — drafting emails, formatting reports, writing reminders — you can probably turn it into a one-command agent prompt. The goal is to skip the boring setup and get straight to the finished draft.

🤖 Example Agents You Can Create

These are ready-to-use prompts you can copy, paste, and start using immediately.

📧

Email Assistant Agent

⚡ Use for Daily
What it does: Drafts professional emails for different audiences — donors, partners, government offices, colleagues. Adjusts tone to match the situation (formal, diplomatic, appreciative, urgent).
When to use: When you need to send an email but want a polished first draft you can review and send.
Draft a professional email to [partner organisation name] acknowledging receipt of their quarterly report. Tone: warm but professional. Mention that we will review it and share feedback within two weeks. Thank them for their continued partnership.
📝

Meeting Summary Agent

⚡ Use for Weekly meetings
What it does: Takes raw notes or a transcript from a meeting and produces a clean, structured summary with key decisions, action items, deadlines, and owners.
When to use: Right after a meeting, before everyone forgets what was agreed on.
Turn these notes into a meeting summary: [Paste your notes here] Format: Title, Date, Attendees, Key Discussion Points, Decisions Made, Action Items (with owner and deadline).
📄

NGO Report Assistant Agent

⚡ Use for Quarterly / Monthly
What it does: Converts raw field notes, data points, and updates into a structured narrative report suitable for donors, board members, or internal use.
When to use: At the end of a project phase, month, or quarter — when you need to compile updates into a readable document.
Create a brief activity report from these points: [Paste your updates here] Structure: 1. Overview (2-3 sentences) 2. Key Activities 3. Achievements & Impact 4. Challenges 5. Next Steps. Language: professional, concise, suitable for an NGO donor report.
🗓️

Event Planning Agent

⚡ Use for Workshops / Trainings
What it does: Generates a complete event preparation checklist: venue, materials, invitations, agenda, speakers, logistics, catering, and post-event tasks.
When to use: As soon as an event is confirmed, to make sure nothing is forgotten.
Create a detailed event preparation checklist for a 1-day capacity-building workshop for 25 participants. Include columns: Task, Deadline, Person Responsible, Status. Cover these areas: venue booking, invitations, materials printing, agenda, speaker coordination, equipment, catering, registration, transportation, and post-event reporting.
📅

Communication Calendar Agent

⚡ Use for Planning
What it does: Creates a month-long or week-long communication plan — which messages go out, to whom, through which channel, and when.
When to use: At the start of a campaign, awareness month, or donor reporting cycle.
Draft a 2-week communication plan for our youth awareness campaign. For each day include: Date, Channel (WhatsApp / Email / LinkedIn / in-person), Audience, Message theme, Goal. Keep messages aligned with our NGO's mission of civic engagement and youth empowerment.
⏰

Follow-up Reminder Agent

⚡ Use for Ongoing
What it does: Tracks pending tasks, follow-ups, and deadlines. Reminds you what needs attention — pending donor replies, unanswered emails, approaching deadlines.
When to use: Every morning to review what needs attention today.
Based on my notes below, list all pending follow-ups that are overdue or due this week, and suggest a short message for each: [Paste your task list or notes here] Organise by priority: Urgent (due today/tomorrow), This week, and Next week. For each, propose a one-sentence follow-up message.
✍️

Document Rewriter Agent

⚡ Use for Polishing
What it does: Takes a draft you've written and improves it — makes it more diplomatic, professional, or accessible depending on the audience. Also handles Arabic/French/English.
When to use: Before sending an important email, publishing a statement, or submitting a report that needs a second pair of eyes.
Rewrite the following paragraph to be more diplomatic and professional. Keep the same meaning but soften the tone: [Paste your text here] Also suggest a version in Arabic if relevant.

📅 Your First 7 Days with Hermes

A gradual plan to go from zero to daily productivity in one week. No pressure — just one small step each day.

Day 1

🚀 Install & Test

Install Hermes and type 3 simple prompts: "Hello", "What can you help me with?", "Write a short thank-you note."

Day 2

📧 Email Assistant

Use the email assistant prompt to draft 2 real emails. Review and send them. Notice how much time it saves on the first draft.

Day 3

📝 Meeting Summary

After your next meeting, paste your notes into Hermes and ask for a clean summary with action items. Share it with the team.

Day 4

📄 Report Template

Create a reusable template for your monthly or quarterly report. Ask Hermes: "Create a report template for our project updates."

Day 5

🗓️ Event Checklist

If you have an upcoming event, ask for a checklist. If not, create a "workshop preparation checklist" and save it for later.

Day 6

⏰ Follow-ups

List your open tasks and pending replies. Ask Hermes to organise them by priority and draft follow-up messages.

Day 7

🔄 Review & Improve

Look back at the week. Which prompts worked best? Save them. Which tasks felt repetitive? Turn them into agents you can reuse.

💡 Tip: After Day 7, pick the one agent that saved you the most time and use it every day for the next week. That's when it becomes a habit.

🔒 Safety & Privacy Guidance

AI is a powerful tool — use it wisely. These guidelines will help you stay safe and professional.

  • 🚫
    Do not paste confidential NGO data unless you have explicit permission. Treat AI conversations the same way you would treat a shared Google Doc — don't put anything you wouldn't want publicly known.
  • 👤
    Avoid sharing private donor or stakeholder information. Names, phone numbers, bank details, personal addresses — redact or anonymise these before pasting into any AI tool.
  • 👁️
    Always review AI outputs before sending. Hermes is very capable, but it can make mistakes — wrong dates, invented facts, slightly off tone. Read everything before it goes out under your name.
  • 🧠
    Use AI as an assistant, not a replacement for your judgment. You are the decision-maker. The agent drafts, suggests, and organises — but you approve, adapt, and decide. Your professional experience is what gives the output value.
  • 🔐
    Keep your API key private. Your API key is like a password — never share it, never email it, never paste it into a public forum.
  • 📌
    When in doubt, ask. If you're unsure whether you can share certain information with Hermes, ask your supervisor or IT officer first.
✅ The safe rule: If you wouldn't write it in an email to a colleague you trust, don't write it in an AI prompt. When used thoughtfully, AI is a force multiplier for good — especially in the NGO space where every hour of saved admin time can go back into mission work.
